## Webhooks Introduction

Webhooks allow you to receive real-time notifications when events occur in your Kottlin Tri documentation. This enables integrations with external services and automated workflows.

## Setting Up Webhooks

Create and configure webhooks in your Kottlin Tri dashboard.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Webhooks" icon="settings">
    Navigate to the webhooks section in your account settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create Webhook" icon="plus">
    Add a new webhook with your endpoint URL.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Events" icon="check">
    Choose which events should trigger the webhook.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test Webhook" icon="send">
    Send a test payload to verify your endpoint.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Webhook Payloads

Understand the structure of webhook payloads.

<CodeGroup tabs="json,yaml">
  ```json
  {
    "event": "documentation.updated",
    "timestamp": "2024-01-15T10:30:00Z",
    "data": {
      "page_id": "introduction",
      "changes": ["content", "metadata"]
    }
  }
  ```
  ```yaml
  event: documentation.updated
  timestamp: "2024-01-15T10:30:00Z"
  data:
    page_id: introduction
    changes:
      - content
      - metadata
  ```
</CodeGroup>

## Troubleshooting Webhooks

Resolve common webhook issues.

<Expandable title="Delivery Failures" default-open="false">
  Check your endpoint URL and ensure it's accessible from the internet.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Signature Mismatch" default-open="false">
  Verify your webhook secret matches the one configured in Kottlin Tri.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Timeout Issues" default-open="false">
  Ensure your endpoint responds within the timeout period (30 seconds).
</Expandable>
